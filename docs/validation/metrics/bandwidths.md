# Formant bandwidths B1 / B2 / B3

> The −3 dB widths (Hz) of the first three formant resonances. Broad
> bandwidths indicate damping / breathiness; narrow ones, a sharply-tuned
> tract. The highest-variance member of the formant family.

## 1. Implementation
- File: `voicemap/metrics.py` → `FormantExtrasCalculator` (lines 1455–1542).
- B1/B2/B3 are the **bandwidths of the same Praat-style Burg poles** that give
  F1/F2/F3 (`_praat_burg_formant_frames`; `bw = −ln|z|·fs/π` in
  `_lpc_to_formants`). They are *not* a separate FWHM pass.
- **Physiological ceiling:** any bandwidth > 800 Hz is zeroed (lines
  1499–1500) — a width that large is not a real formant (it is a mis-located
  or merged pole). `0` follows the project-wide "NA-as-0" convention.
- Each cycle takes its enclosing 10 ms frame's bandwidths.

## 2. Reference Standard
- **Praat** (Boersma & Weenink), `Sound: To Formant (burg)` →
  `Get bandwidth at time` — the pole-radius bandwidth of the same Burg LPC.
- Compared against `parselmouth` `Formant.get_bandwidth_at_time(n, t)` from
  the identical `to_formant_burg(...)` configuration used for F1–F3.
- **Tolerance:** `conventions.md §1` sets bandwidths at `rtol = 0.10` (10 %)
  because they are intrinsically high-variance. The acceptance is on the
  **aggregate median**, not per-cycle (see §4/§7).

## 4. Validation Method
- **(A) Aggregate-median parity** vs Praat `Get bandwidth at time`, on the
  real 10 s voice: `|median(ours) − median(Praat)| / median(Praat) ≤ 10 %`
  for each of B1, B2, B3 (comparing only cycles where both are in-band,
  ≤ 800 Hz). The median is the reported clinical/statistical summary; the
  per-cycle scatter is much larger and is documented, not gated.
- **(B) Behavioural ground truth:**
  - the 800 Hz ceiling holds (no surviving bandwidth exceeds it);
  - silence → no bandwidths.
- **(C) Real-corpus behaviour** — deferred to the corpus phase.

## 7. Known Limitations
- **High per-cycle scatter.** Although the medians track Praat to ~1–5 %, the
  *per-cycle* median |Δf/f| is ≈ 10 % (B1), ≈ 17 % (B2), ≈ 11 % (B3).
  Bandwidth is the formant quantity most sensitive to LPC order, window
  length, and the exact pole radius, so cycle-to-cycle disagreement with
  Praat is expected and intrinsic — not a VoiceMap defect. Only aggregate
  statistics (median over a region) should be read clinically.
- **B2 is the least stable** — ~33 % of its per-cycle values are zeroed by the
  800 Hz ceiling on this recording (the second pole frequently merges/spreads),
  so B2's effective sample size is smaller. Use B1/B3 preferentially.
- **The 800 Hz clear is a hard gate, not a taper** — a genuinely broad but
  real formant (very breathy voice) could be zeroed. Acceptable for the sung-
  voice target; flagged so a 0 is read as "not measured", not "0 Hz".
- Shares the formant tracker's `max_formant = 5500 Hz` and high-F0 synthetic
  confound (see `formants.md §7`); no synthetic bandwidth GT is used.
