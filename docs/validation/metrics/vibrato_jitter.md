# VibratoJitter — vibrato-period stability

> Coefficient of variation (%) of the vibrato *period* (1/rate) inside a
> sliding window of cycles. Low = a steady, even vibrato; high = a wobbly,
> irregular one. Complements VibratoRate / VibratoExtent (which say *how fast*
> and *how wide* the vibrato is) with *how steady* it is.

## 1. Implementation
- File: `voicemap/metrics.py` → `VibratoJitterCalculator` (lines 1618–1656).
- Window `W = min(window_cycles=40, max(n//4, 4))`. For each centred window,
  take the cycles with `rate > 0`, form `periods = 1/rate`, and emit
  `100·std(periods)/mean(periods)` at the window centre `i + W//2`. Needs ≥ 4
  valid cycles in a window; edge cycles outside the centred region stay 0.
- Input is the per-cycle `vibrato_rate` from `VibratoCalculator`.
- Registry key `VibratoJitter` (Singing, `待验证=True`).

## 2. Reference Standard
- No external tool. Vibrato-rate regularity (jitter of the vibrato cycle) is a
  recognised voice-quality dimension (e.g. Dejonckere; Sundberg's work on
  vibrato), but the exact CV-of-period formula is self-defined, so the
  reference is the **closed-form CV** of a designed rate sequence:
  - constant rate → CV = 0;
  - period alternating `p0(1 ± δ)` → CV = `100·δ` exactly.

## 7. Known Limitations
- **Compressed magnitude for realistic vibrato.** The vibrato rate cannot
  change faster than the vibrato oscillation itself (~6 Hz, ~0.17 s/period), so
  within the ~40-cycle (~0.2 s) window the period CV stays small. A clearly
  wobbling 3.5–8.5 Hz vibrato reads ~7 %, a steady one ~0.2 %: the metric
  *separates* steady from wobbly reliably, but the absolute % is not a direct
  read-out of rate scatter.
- **Inherits VibratoCalculator's resolution.** It consumes the per-cycle
  `vibrato_rate`; any bias or quantisation there (the FFT bin grid, the 80-cycle
  window) propagates into the period CV. The ~0.2 % noise floor on a perfectly
  steady vibrato is exactly that estimator jitter.
- **Edge cycles are 0 by design.** Cycles outside the centred-window region are
  left at 0 (not back-filled) to avoid the VRP vertical-bar artifact that the
  old "copy nearest value" behaviour caused (see the calculator comment).
- **(C) corpus** (pop's narrow/fast vs bel-canto's even vibrato) deferred —
  needs a singing corpus.
