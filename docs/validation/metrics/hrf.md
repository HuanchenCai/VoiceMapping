# HRFegg — EGG Harmonic Richness Factor

> The energy of the EGG harmonics (H2…HN) relative to the fundamental, in dB.
> Richer harmonics ↔ a sharper, more abrupt vocal-fold contact (pressed); a
> near-sinusoidal EGG (breathy) has low HRF.

## 1. Implementation
- File: `voicemap/metrics.py` → `HRFCalculator` (lines 667–689).
- Per cycle: `_compute_cycle_dft(egg, idx, n_harmonics)` → harmonic magnitudes;
  `fund = |X1|`, `harms = 2·√(Σ_{k≥2} |Xk|²)`,
  `HRFegg = 20·log10(harms / fund)`. `n_harmonics = 10`.

## 2. Reference Standard
- **Howard (1998)** — EGG harmonic richness; the project's SuperCollider
  `namePhasePortrait` lineage. No SC source available to parity against, so
  validation uses the **closed form** of the ratio on a synthetic
  multi-harmonic signal (signal-agnostic: the calculator is a per-cycle DFT
  ratio, so a known-amplitude harmonic stack gives the exact HRF).

## 3. Test Signals
Synthetic harmonic stacks (in-harness, F0 = 150 Hz): `Σ_k A_k·cos(2π·k·f0·t)`
with known `(A1, A2, A3)` ∈ {(1,.5,.25), (1,1,1), (1,.7,.5)}, plus a pure
fundamental (no harmonics).

## 4. Validation Method
- **(B) Synthetic ground truth:** `HRFegg` recovers
  `20·log10(2·√(Σ_{k≥2} A_k²)/A1)` (max |Δ| ≤ 0.1 dB); a pure fundamental reads
  ≪ 0 dB (no harmonic energy).
- **(A)** SC `namePhasePortrait` parity unavailable (no SC source) — the closed
  form is the stronger anchor. **(C)** corpus deferred.

## 7. Known Limitations
- **Floor at very low harmonics.** A near-sinusoidal EGG gives `harms → 0`,
  floored at 1e-15 → HRF ≈ −265 dB (numerical, not −∞). Read as "no harmonic
  richness", not a calibrated value.
- **Capped at `n_harmonics = 10`.** Energy above the 10th harmonic is excluded;
  for a very sharp closure with significant high harmonics, HRF is slightly
  under-counted. Adequate at the EGG sample rate / pitch range used.
- **Depends on per-cycle DFT** — accuracy follows correct cycle marking; a
  mis-marked cycle smears the harmonic amplitudes.
- **No SC parity.** The `namePhasePortrait` provenance is not cross-checked
  (no SC source); the closed-form synthetic GT validates the formula, not the
  exact SC numerical convention.
