# Formants F1 / F2 / F3

> The three lowest vocal-tract resonances (Hz). Vowel identity, vocal-tract
> length, and singing-formant tuning all read off F1–F3.

## 1. Implementation
- File: `voicemap/metrics.py`
  - `FormantCalculator` (lines 1363–1460) → F1/F2/F3 (+ SFE).
  - `_praat_burg_formant_frames` (lines 1300–1360): a faithful translation of
    Praat **`Sound: To Formant (burg)`** — resample to `2·max_formant` →
    pre-emphasis (50 Hz) → Praat Gaussian window (physical length = 2× the
    25 ms window) → Burg LPC of order `2·n_formants` → polynomial roots.
  - `_lpc_to_formants` (lines 1260–1297): for each pole `z`,
    `freq = |∠z|·fs/2π`, `bw = −ln|z|·fs/π`; keep one pole per conjugate pair
    in `[50, max_formant]`, sorted ascending → F1, F2, F3.
- Defaults: `n_formants=5`, `max_formant=5500 Hz`, `win=25 ms`, `hop=10 ms`,
  `pre_emphasis=50 Hz`. Each cycle takes its enclosing frame's formants.

## 2. Reference Standard
- **Praat** (Boersma & Weenink), `Sound: To Formant (burg)`
  (`fon/Sound_to_Formant.cpp`); the split-Levinson / Burg LPC + root solver.
  Underlying: Markel & Gray, *Linear Prediction of Speech* (1976).
- Compared against `parselmouth`
  `to_formant_burg(time_step=0.01, max_number_of_formants=5,
  maximum_formant=5500, window_length=0.025, pre_emphasis_from=50)`.
- The acceptance bar (per PLAN) is **numerical parity with Praat**,
  `|Δf/f| < 5 %` — formants have no absolute ground truth on real voice, and
  the synthetic Klatt values are confounded (see §4/§7).

## 3. Test Signals

**Synthetic** (`docs/validation/test_signals/`):

  Used **only for F1** corroboration; F2/F3 are confounded (§7).

## 4. Validation Method
- **(A) Numerical parity** vs Praat `to_formant_burg`, on the real 10 s voice:
  for each of F1, F2, F3, sample Praat at every cycle time and compute the
  per-cycle relative difference; require **median `|Δf/f| ≤ 5 %`**.
- **(B) Synthetic ground truth (F1 only)**: median F1 over the steady part of
  the `a` and `e` segments within **12 %** of the imposed Klatt F1. F2/F3 are
  deliberately excluded — at F0 = 150–200 Hz the voicing harmonics sit on top
  of the formants and pull the LPC poles for *both* trackers, so the Klatt
  nominal F2/F3 is not a reliable target (Praat misreads them too).
- **(C) Real-corpus behaviour** — deferred to the corpus phase.

Reproducible steps:
```bash
python scripts/validate_metric.py formants
```

## 7. Known Limitations
- **Synthetic F2/F3 are not a usable ground truth.** On `vowel_formants_a_e_i`
  (F0 = 150 Hz) the order-10 LPC locks onto strong voicing harmonics (e.g. a
  spurious ~768 Hz pole on the `a` vowel), shifting the F2/F3 labels. Praat
  shows the same family of errors (it reads `a` F3 ≈ 1914 Hz vs nominal 2440),
  so this is a property of LPC formant estimation at high F0, not a VoiceMap
  defect. Real connected voice (lower effective harmonic density per formant)
  does not trigger it — there the per-cycle Praat parity is 1–2 % (§5 A).
- **Per-cycle assignment adds variance at transitions.** A formant is a frame
  quantity (25 ms) assigned to ~5 ms cycles, so cycles spanning a vowel
  transition carry the frame value; the P75 |Δf/f| (≈ 9 % on F2) is higher
  than the median (≈ 2 %) for this reason. Medians are the reported statistic.
- **Above `max_formant = 5500 Hz` formants are not tracked**; F4+ is out of
  scope (only F1–F3 ship).
- **B1/B2/B3 come from a different tracker** (`FormantExtrasCalculator`), so
  bandwidths are validated separately — see `bandwidths.md`.
